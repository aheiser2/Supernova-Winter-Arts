Frequently Asked Questions

  • WHEN ARE AUDITIONS?

    This year, auditions take place over two days, both of which are required to audition:

    • 11/16/25 - 1pm - 5pm
    • 11/23/25 - 1pm - 5pm

  • HOW DO I REGISTER TO ATTEND THE AUDITIONS?

    To register, you must complete and submit the online registration form found here:

    2025 Registration Form

  • HOW MUCH DOES IT COST TO AUDITION?

    This year, auditions are free!

  • WHAT DO AUDITIONS CONSIST OF?

    Auditions usually take place in November. First we have an audition clinic so that auditionees can learn skills needed for the audition itself, as well as for our coaches to get to know people and see their interpersonal skills. During the second week, after a couple hours learning/relearning the audition routine, we will have the actual audition portion which consists of performing the routine in front of the coaches. Once auditions are complete, you will receive an email about your membership in a few days.

  • WHEN AND HOW WILL I BE NOTIFIED IF I HAVE MADE SUPERNOVA FOR THE SEASON?

    Supernova will contact everyone via email within 2-5 days after auditions.

  • DO YOU ACCEPT VIDEO AUDITIONS?

    It is important that we see how you interact with others to determine if you are a good fit for the guard. Because of this, we will not accept video auditions.

  • WHAT IF I CAN'T MAKE IT TO AUDITIONS, BUT WOULD STILL LIKE TO AUDITION?

    Contact Supernova Winter Arts atsupernovawinterarts@gmail.comto discuss.

  • DO I NEED TO ATTEND THE AUDITION CLINIC TO AUDITION?

    Yes… although in very strict circumstances, we will make an exception. If you have previously worked with either of the coaches and therefore, they know you well enough to determine if your personality is a good fit, you can reach out about missing the first weekend (this will still put you at a potential disadvantage for the following audition weekend if we start teaching the routine at the audition clinic).

  • CAN I BRING A GUEST TO WATCH ME AUDITION?

    This is a closed audition. Guests must stay outside the gym.

  • WHAT SHOULD I BRING WITH ME TO AUDITIONS?

    • Completed and signed Participation Waiver (must be signed by a parent/guardian if you are under 18)
    • Equipment, unless you've notified us you need to borrow
    • Water bottle/snacks
    • A great attitude and willingness to learn ;)
    • Your passion and desire for performing
    • Hand sanitizer/sanitizer wipes
    • Protective gear you may need such as knee pads or gloves

  • WHAT SHOULD I WEAR TO AUDITION AND REHEARSALS?

    We request that you wear comfortable, black, form-fitting dance/athletic attire that allows you to move easily while also allowing the staff to see the line of your body during dance. Please dress in layers so that you can remove heavier articles of clothing, as your body warms. You may wear dance shoes or dance in your bare feet. Hair must be pulled back and out of the face in a ponytail or bun, and jewelry is not to be worn.

  • WHO WILL BE TEACHING/JUDGING THE AUDITIONS?

    Members of the winter guard staff (your potential coaches!).

  • DO I NEED PRIOR GUARD OR DANCE EXPERIENCE TO AUDITION?

    While it will certainly help, it is not required that you have done guard or dance before. Everyone has to start somewhere, and maybe you'll take to it like a fish to water!

  • IS IT REQUIRED TO BE SKILLED ON RIFLE AND/OR SABRE TO BE ABLE TO AUDITION?

    Not at all! These are skills that are season/show dependent, and typically learned by more advanced members. If we decide to use these pieces of equipment, we will teach it during the season.

  • SHOULD I AUDITION IF I AM INVOLVED IN OTHER ACTIVITIES?

    That depends on your own ability to balance your time well - and the only person that knows that is you. Attendance at all rehearsals and performances is mandatory for all members. Even if you are working, attending school, involved in another school activity, or having family functions, one must understand that you are making a season-long commitment to be a performer with Supernova, and missing time from practice and performances is detrimental to the team's success. Time management of your education, activities, family, and social life must allow you to dedicate the required week nights and weekends to the team. In the interest of being understanding, we will allow ONE regular season (NOT Championships) missed performance for another activity. If you have prior commitments that will cause you to miss more than one competitive performance, you should reconsider your membership for a later season. Failure to adhere to this policy will result you in being demoted to a Swing, or potentially full removal from the team. Part of the audition process involves filling out a conflict sheet so that we can see if your schedule will work with ours.

  • SHOULD I AUDITION IF I AM NOT IN SHAPE/NOT FLEXIBLE?

    Yes! While being flexible and in good physical form already will certainly help, you will get stronger, more flexible, and better endurance over the course of the season. The biggest things we're looking for at auditions are your ability to pick-up/learn new skills and choreography, your work ethic, and your ability to be a team player.

  • SHOULD I AUDITION IF I HAVE A MEDICAL CONDITION?

    Supernova is a non-discriminatory organization and while we encourage the participation of students of all backgrounds, we strongly recommend that students not audition if they have severe medical issues due to the very physical nature of the activity. You know yourself best - if your condition will hinder your ability to keep up with the team or put you (or others) in danger if it acts up during a performance, it might be best to be a part of the team as a volunteer helper.

  • DO I NEED TO LIVE IN LINCOLN TO MARCH?

    No. While you will need to attend all rehearsals throughout the season, you do not have to live in Lincoln to do so.

  • CAN I BE A COLLEGE STUDENT AND A MEMBER OF SUPERNOVA AT THE SAME TIME?

    Absolutely; many winter guard members around the US are currently college students and/or working part time or full time jobs. It is a commitment and will require a lot of time management, but it is definitely possible. It comes down to you knowing how well you can balance your time.

  • CAN I BE A HIGH SCHOOL STUDENT AND A MEMBER OF SUPERNOVA AT THE SAME TIME? CAN I PERFORM WITH SUPERNOVA IF I AM ALREADY PARTICIPATING IN MY HIGH SCHOOL WINTERGUARD PROGRAM?

    Winter Guard International Policy reads:

    “WGI discourages independent color guards from utilizing membership composed of any high school students who were previously enrolled in an active winter guard high school program and have not yet graduated.”

    We will in no way recruit students from local high school programs that have a Scholastic Winter Guard. We strongly believe this rule fosters the growth of many local high school winter guard programs. Exceptions can be made if your high school does not have a competitive winter guard program (currently Standing Bear is the only one that does - reach out to their coach here: dbuckend@lps.org).

  • ARE THERE AGE LIMITS FOR MEMBERSHIP?

    According to WGI, to march in Independent A performers can not be over 22 years of age as of 12:01 on April 1st of the given year. We allow members to audition if they have reached the age of 14 by September 1st prior to the season starting.

  • WHAT IS THE SCHEDULE? WHEN DOES THE SEASON START AND HOW LONG DOES IT RUN?

    Click here for the 2026 Calendar. Winter Guard practice will start Monday, December 1st. Typically practices will be every Monday and Wednesday night from 6-8:30 pm. We often have an extended couple days of practice during winter break, depending on people's schedules, and then try to have at least an hour of rehearsal before leaving for competitions. Finals in our circuit are on April 4th, but we will have a Showcase with the other Lincoln winter guards sometime during the weeks following, depending on the groups' schedules. We anticipate being done before the start of May.

  • WHAT IS THE TIME COMMITMENT?

    It varies from week to week, and depends on the time of the season. Every week expect to have at least 5 hours (2.5 on Monday and 2.5 on Wednesday), as well as time you are putting into practice on your own (this is an expectation in order for the whole team to succeed). We expect to run at least one fundraiser each month, which might take anywhere from 1 to 10 hours depending on what they are. Competition days (roughly 5 per season) are about 10 hour days, from practice beforehand to the end of awards.

  • WHAT DOES REHEARSAL LOOK LIKE?

    Rehearsal will vary from day to day depending on what is needed, but will usually somewhat follow this format:

    • Stretching
    • Flag warmup
    • Across-the-floors/dance technique
    • Learn/cleaning routine/drill

    There is typically at least one if not two 5-10 minute breaks each night, where we might do a game/bonding activity. These are usually about every 50 minutes to break up rehearsal/give performers a chance to rest.

  • WHERE WILL REHEARSALS BE LOCATED?

    Supernova will be having a majority of its rehearsals in the Lincoln area for the 2026 season. Exact locations will be provided after acceptance to the team.

  • HOW OFTEN WILL WE PERFORM?

    We will perform at 4 regular season competitions, and also the Heartland Winter Arts Association Championships; all of these typically are in Omaha or the surrounding towns (actual locations are still being decided on by the circuit). We plan to have a post-competition season Showcase with the other two Lincoln teams somewhere in Lincoln (UNL and Standing Bear). There may be other performances beginning in mid-January, depending on opportunity - we will keep performers updated as we find out performance chances.

  • WHAT DOES A COMPETITION DAY TYPICALLY LOOK LIKE?

    Competition days typically start out with an hour practice in Lincoln, then traveling to the competition site. We aim to arrive at least an hour before our scheduled performance time, although depending on start time, this may not always be possible. Once we are there, we go through a warm-up /practice timeline that leads up to our performance, which lasts about 7 minutes. Depending on our scheduled performance time, we may watch some of the other guards either before or after our performance. We may also stay for awards, depending on our scheduled performance time.

  • HOW MUCH ARE ANNUAL DUES? HOW MUCH DOES IT COST?

    Unfortunately, we won't have a specific cost calculated until we know how many members we'll have. A few things that affect costs are:

    • Uniforms
    • Flags/equipment/props
    • Flags/equipment/props
    • Music Licensing
    • Circuit/competition fees
    • Rehearsal space rental

    To give a rough estimate, Millard North's Varsity Guard's dues are around $950. Many independent guards can range from $1000 to as much $3000 per season. We plan to keep costs as low as we can, and expect that costs would be closer to Millard North's.

  • WHAT IS NOT COVERED IN MEMBERSHIP FEES?

    Not included in the membership fees: Travel to and from contest/rehearsals, jackets, merchandise booth items, shoes, gloves, make-up, and undergarments needed for performance. Meals on competition days will be at the member's own expense.

  • DO I HAVE TO PAY ALL MY FEES UPFRONT?

    We offer two options: full payment, or a monthly payment plan so that you do not have to pay everything at once. However, as an incentive, if you pay the full cost upfront we will take $50 off your fees for the season.

  • WHAT IF I AM OFFERED A SWING POSITION?

    Swings are required to pay full amount in dues, but if not offered a position within the performance by the end of the season, 50% of the dues are returned to you at the end of the season. You are still able to pay on a monthly payment plan.

  • ARE THERE WAYS TO HELP COVER MY COSTS?

    Yes! You will receive your first fundraising program as soon as you are contracted for the 2026 Season to assist you in paying down your dues as quickly as possible. Our fundraising programs offer the ability to pay down your dues speedily and take the strain and stress out of paying the majority of your payments. We are also planning several fundraisers to help offset costs, including a couple of restaurant nights, wrapping paper night, a bingo/raffle night, as well as a few others. All members are required to participate in team fundraisers.

    If you have concerns about being able to pay, please come talk to us! We don't want anyone to miss out due to finances, and will do our best to figure something out.

  • HOW DO I PAY MY MEMBER FEES?

    Member fees can be paid online or in person via cash or check. There will be more details in your member handbook.

  • CAN I BE REMOVED FROM THE TEAM OR DEMOTED?

    Unfortunately, yes. The following behaviors are not tolerated, and will result in either being demoted to a swing with no change in member fees, or complete removal from the team with no refunds on fees paid:

    • Missing multiple rehearsals
    • Missing a single rehearsal/performance without any kind of head's up to the coaching staff
    • Missing more than the one allowed performance
    • Not paying member fees in a timely manner (or at all)
    • Not practicing outside of rehearsal to maintain a similar level of growth as the rest of the group, or to catch up on missed rehearsals.
    • Intentionally damaging equipment or rehearsal space, or not respecting rehearsal space in a way that could result in the loss of our ability to continue using it.
    • Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol at a Supernova rehearsal, performance, or other event
    • Possessing or distributing alcohol or a prohibited substance at a Supernova rehearsal, performance, or other event, or to another winter guard participant regardless of location.
    • Bullying, abuse, or other harassment directed at team members, coaches/staff, or other winter guards. This can include but is not limited to: physical abuse such as hitting, pushing, beating, biting, or other offensive contact; verbal abuse such as ridiculing, taunting, name-calling, intimidating, or threatening to cause harm; or social abuse such as rumors or false statements to diminish someone's reputation or socially excluding someone and asking others to do the same. We will do our best to address first time instances through communicating with both parties to understand, but repeated issues will result in removal from the team.
    • Behavior that is harmful, demeaning, annoying, or causes fear or discomfort aimed at people's age, race, ethnicity, sex, gender, orientation, culture, religion, national origin, mental or physical disability.
    • Inappropriate fraternizing, such as sexual or romantic relationships between older participants and minor participants, or any relationship that puts any party at risk of harm.
    • Sexual or gender-related harassment such as unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, unwanted and continued sexual flirtations, and propositions. It also includes sexually degrading words and other conduct of a sexual nature. This also includes nonconsensual sexual contact, exposing a minor to sexual content/imagery, and taking non-consensual or abusive sexual advantage of another for one's own advantage.

    We reserve the right to demote or remove members for issues not listed above if we feel that the behavior is detrimental to the team or our organization.

  • WHAT IF I DECIDE I DON'T WANT TO DO WINTER GUARD AFTER THE SEASON STARTS?

    This is a team sport that only succeeds if EVERYONE on the team is committed and doing their best to achieve that success. This is why it is VERY important that you ask yourself before the season begins if you are up for the challenge and ready to make that commitment. It is frustrating for the staff to have to rewrite choreography and drill, and it is frustrating for your teammates to have to relearn things if you drop out. It's frustrating for potential members who wanted to be on the team that missed out on the chance because a limited spot was given to you instead. You know yourself best - if you already don't think you have the time/energy/headspace to take this on, don't try-out.

    If you do get accepted to the team and realize you don't want to do it anymore, please come talk to a staff member about your reason(s) so we can see if there is any solution to them. If you are still committed to dropping out after discussing with a staff member, you will be released from the team with NO refund on any member fees paid so far, but will be exempt from paying anything further. Depending on your reason, this may bar you from auditioning at Supernova in the future.
